  • The best place to help plan your trip to London, is the Trip Advisor London Forum. As you have seen, I used it exclusively and even met some friends on there. I cannot express how helpful and gracious that forum is. Try to Google things first, obviously, but if you're stumped or want the forum to look at your plans and tell if it seems feasible to do in a day or two or whatever, they are the best!
  • I know we can and do use our phones for everything these days, but if you like actual maps or want a back-up in case something happens to your phone, check out this guide book - it is awesome. It includes underground and bus information which I found to be so rewarding.üìñ Map Booklet
  • Do not be intimidated by the underground or bus serice. If you're trying to figure out the tube and it's not making sense, all I can say is - it will when you get there. It is what I was told before my first time and it is the truest advice you can get. It's hard to describe, but once it clicks, you'll understand. The Travel for London (TfL) site is a plethora of information. Spend some time on there before you leave on your trip.
  • Please, don't stop in the middle of the sidewalk to have a meeting or take photos or look at your maps. You have to remember that London is home to a lot of people, first and foremost. They are all around you just trying to get to work or home or to the pub. Be kind, move to the side first so that pedestrian traffic can continue to flow.
  • When using the undergound, on the escalators - unless you would rather run up and down the escalator stairs, stand to your RIGHT. This allows people who want to run up and down to be able to do so. If you forget, you will here "Behind you" or even "on your left" - which is proper British for - move to the right.
  • Keep a digital copy of your passport
  • Try to carry a reusable water bottle, London has plenty of places you can fill it up
  • Download offline maps before you go - just in case!
